Your Own Website vs. Social Media: Why Both Matter, But Only One of Them Truly Belongs to You

📅 February 2025 ⏱ 6 min read

"I do not need a website, I have Instagram." I have heard that sentence more times over the past few years than I can count. Usually it comes from businesses that are genuinely good at creating content and therefore believe that is enough. Spoiler: it is not. And the reason is simpler than you think.

The core problem: you are building on someone else's land

Imagine opening a retail store, but instead of merely renting the space, you also give the landlord full control over your opening hours, your storefront and whether anyone even walks past your shop. Sounds absurd? That is exactly what you are doing when Instagram is your only online presence.

Instagram, TikTok and Facebook are all platforms with their own rules, their own algorithms and their own business interests. Your account can be suspended tomorrow, your reach can be cut in half, or the entire platform can face regulatory restrictions. And you have zero control over any of it.

This is not a theoretical risk. It has happened repeatedly. Accounts with tens of thousands of followers have been suspended overnight. Algorithms changed and organic reach collapsed. TikTok was temporarily blocked in some countries. Anyone who built everything there suddenly had nothing left.

What you get with your own website

🌐 Own website

  • You own the content
  • No algorithm decides your visibility
  • Google can find you long term
  • No competitor can advertise next to your profile
  • Full control over design and branding
  • Direct access to customer data in a GDPR compliant way

📱 Social media

  • The content belongs to the platform
  • Reach depends on the algorithm
  • Short lived, posts disappear in the feed
  • Competitor ads can appear right next to you
  • Design is controlled by the platform
  • No real customer list of your own

But social media still matters, right?

Yes, absolutely. It is just meant for something different. Social media is ideal for reach, attention, community building and first contact. A website is where trust is built and buying decisions happen.

The formula that works is simple: social media brings people in, your website turns them into customers. If you only have social media, you are not giving people anywhere meaningful to go. If you only have a website, you may not know how to attract new people in the first place. The real game is using both together.

Use social media as a traffic source

Every post you publish should keep one question in mind: where do interested people go next? A link in your bio is not enough. Give people a concrete reason to click through to your website. Exclusive content, a contact form, a service overview, anything they cannot get directly on the platform itself.

Use your website as the trust anchor

When someone hears about you for the first time, whether through Instagram, a recommendation or Google, they will almost always visit your website before reaching out. That is the moment when they decide whether they want to work with you or your business. A good website answers that question with a yes.

What this means in practice

You do not need to choose. You need both, but you need to understand what each one is actually for. Social media is advertising. Your website is your business. Invest accordingly.

If today you are only active on social media, start small. A simple, clear website with your services, a contact option and a solid first impression is better than none at all. The perfect time was yesterday. The next best time is now.
